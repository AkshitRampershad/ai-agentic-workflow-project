Product Specification: Email Router

Overview:
The Email Router is an intelligent email triage system that automatically classifies inbound customer and internal emails, extracts key metadata, and routes each message to the correct team or workflow queue. The goal is to reduce manual triage effort, improve response time, and ensure high-priority messages are not missed.

Target Users:
- Customer Support Representatives
- Operations Managers
- Technical Project Managers
- Engineering Support Teams

Core Problems:
- Emails are manually reviewed and forwarded, causing delays and inconsistent routing.
- Urgent customer issues can be missed or assigned to the wrong team.
- Managers lack visibility into routing accuracy, backlog volume, and SLA risks.

Goals:
- Classify email intent and urgency.
- Route emails to the correct team queue.
- Extract key fields such as sender, account, topic, priority, attachments, and requested action.
- Provide audit logs for routing decisions.
- Allow administrators to configure routing rules.
- Provide dashboards for routing volume, SLA status, and exception queues.

Functional Requirements:
1. Ingest inbound emails from a connected mailbox or API source.
2. Parse subject, body, sender, recipients, timestamps, and attachments metadata.
3. Classify emails into categories such as billing, technical support, account management, sales, compliance, spam, and general inquiry.
4. Determine priority levels: low, medium, high, urgent.
5. Apply business rules to route emails to team queues.
6. Flag uncertain classifications for human review.
7. Store routing decisions, confidence scores, and timestamps in an audit log.
8. Notify assigned teams when urgent emails arrive.
9. Allow administrators to create, edit, enable, and disable routing rules.
10. Provide reporting dashboards for routing accuracy, queue volume, SLA breaches, and manual override trends.

Non-Functional Requirements:
- Classification response time should be under 3 seconds per email for standard volume.
- System should support at least 10,000 emails per day in the pilot.
- Routing decisions must be explainable and auditable.
- Sensitive email content must be handled securely.
- The system must provide fallback handling when classification confidence is low.
- The system should be modular enough to add new routing categories later.

Success Metrics:
- Reduce manual triage effort by at least 40%.
- Improve urgent email identification accuracy to at least 90%.
- Reduce average first-response delay by 25%.
- Maintain routing audit coverage for 100% of processed emails.
